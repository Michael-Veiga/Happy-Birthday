And Now a Super Corny Poem!

Searching for love in a stifling cinder,
suffering the heat a long lonely lindor,
swiped through a vast ocean of coals,
scorching his shoes with piercing red holes,

Suddenly a witch emerged from the ember,
bestowing the lindor with boots made of timber,
She said I'm Hermione your love everlasting,
Now give me some ribs or i'll use my spellcasting,

The lindor rejoiced and sought out a swine,
but all to be found were chips and moonshine,
perched precariously on a great thirsty moose,
while they drank he admired the witch's caboose,

As the pair relished their open debut,
she talked excitedly of a strange brew,
a love potion perhaps he could feel in his face,
Sweetly she laughed not a drink but a place,

Enchanted they flew with hearts light as air,
to find love so pure is exceedingly rare,
finding himself overflowing with bliss,
he sought to bestow the witch with a kiss,

She blushed and she dodged and she muttered a spell,
and told the old lindor this wouldn't end well,
but even a man not six inches tall,
could tell she was hiding her heart from it all,

So they laughed and they joked and they sat for a while,
until the young witch became trapped in her smile,
and when the old lindor returned to his height,
the witch let him kiss her all into the night.
